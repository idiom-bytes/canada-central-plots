Crime Severity Index by Province

Source: Statistics Canada, Table 35-10-0026-01

The Crime Severity Index (CSI) measures the overall volume and severity of police-reported crime. Unlike simple crime rates that count all offences equally, the CSI assigns higher weights to more serious crimes. The index is benchmarked to 100 in 2006 for Canada as a whole. A value above 100 means crime severity is higher than the 2006 national baseline; below 100 means it is lower. The CSI captures both changes in the number of crimes and shifts in the types of crimes being committed.
