Canadian Government Deficit / Surplus

Source: Department of Finance, Fiscal Reference Tables 2025

The annual budgetary balance measures how much more (deficit) or less (surplus) a government spent than it collected in revenue during a single fiscal year. A deficit means the government borrowed to cover the shortfall; a surplus means it had money left over to pay down debt or save. This is a flow measure, capturing the gap for one year only, not the total debt outstanding. For total accumulated debt, see Net Debt.
