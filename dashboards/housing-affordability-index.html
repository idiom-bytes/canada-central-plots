Housing Affordability Index

Source: StatCan Table 18-10-0205 (NHPI) & Table 14-10-0064 (Wages)

How fast are new home prices growing relative to wages? This index measures the ratio of housing price growth to wage growth, both indexed to 100 in the year 2000. A value above 100 means prices have grown faster than wages (less affordable). Below 100 means wages have outpaced prices (more affordable). Manitoba's index at 154 means prices have grown 54% faster than wages since 2000.

Baseline = 100 (year 2000). Above 100 = less affordable. Below 100 = more affordable.
