Population Growth by Province

Source: Statistics Canada, Table 17-10-0005-01

Annual population growth rate measures the year-over-year percentage change in a province's total population. Population growth is driven by three components: natural increase (births minus deaths), international migration, and interprovincial migration. Provinces with strong economic opportunities tend to attract both international immigrants and residents from other provinces, while declining industries can trigger population outflows. Recent years have seen record-breaking national growth driven by immigration policy, with effects distributed unevenly across provinces.
