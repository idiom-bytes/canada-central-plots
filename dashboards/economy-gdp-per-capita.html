GDP Per Capita by Province

Source: Statistics Canada, Table 36-10-0222-01 & Table 17-10-0005-01

Real GDP per capita measures the total economic output of a region divided by its population, adjusted for inflation (chained 2017 dollars). It is the standard measure of economic productivity and living standards. Higher GDP per capita generally means more economic activity per person, though it does not capture how that wealth is distributed. Resource-rich provinces like Alberta and Saskatchewan consistently outperform, while Atlantic provinces trail the national average. Comparing provinces over time reveals whether the gap is widening or closing.
