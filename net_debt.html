Canadian Government Net Debt

Source: Department of Finance, Fiscal Reference Tables 2025

Net debt is the total amount a government owes after subtracting its financial assets (cash, investments, loans receivable) from its total liabilities (bonds, pensions, accounts payable). It represents the stock of debt at a point in time, the accumulated result of every past deficit, surplus, revaluation, and accounting change. Unlike the annual deficit, which measures a single year's shortfall, net debt shows the full balance sheet position. It is the standard measure used by credit rating agencies and fiscal policy analysts to assess a government's long-term fiscal health.
